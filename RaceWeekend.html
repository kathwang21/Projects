RACE WEEKEND

Life is about the journey,
not the destination.



  • Headquarters for the Marathon is in Monterey, 120 miles south of San Francisco, 320 miles north of Los Angeles. You can fly directly into Monterey Airport, only five minutes from downtown Monterey.
  • Other airport options include San Jose, (70 miles away), San Francisco and Oakland (110 miles). Transportation from San Jose and San Francisco airports is provided by Monterey Airbus, 831.373.7777
  • For a map providing driving distances from various CA cities to Big Sur, as well as driving distances from nearby airports to the Monterey Peninsula, click HERE.
  • If you are participating in the Marathon (includes Boston 2 Big Sur & Runner's World Challenge), the 21-Miler, or the 10.6-Miler, you will need to get a free bus ticket at the Health & Fitness Expo when you pick up your race packet. Buses depart from various locations on the Monterey Peninsula. You will be assigned a departure location based on where you are staying race weekend and the number of buses at each location. Please see below for departure times, locations, and links to maps. These maps will also be in your Race Weekend Instructions (emailed to participants and available mid-April) and available at the Expo.
  • If you are staying in Big Sur, there will be a shuttle making a continuous loop between River Inn to the north and Deetjen's to the south. The shuttle will run from 4:45 AM to 6:00 AM. Runners wishing to take this shuttle should stand on the side of Highway One where they will be visible to the drivers. If you are staying in Big Sur and doing an event other than the marathon, you must still take the shuttle to the marathon start. At the marathon start, you will disembark and get on a different northbound bus

    to be transported to the start of your event (21-Miler, 10.6-Miler, Relay legs 2 - 5, 12K or 5K). Plan on arriving at the marathon start no later than 6 AM.

  • The 12K and 5K are out-and-back courses from the finish area in Carmel. Entrants in the 12K and 5K are responsible for their own transportation to the start of their event at the Marathon finish (Highway One and Rio Road) unless staying in Big Sur (see paragraph above). Parking is available at the Crossroads Carmel shopping center and at the Barnyard Shopping Village one block north of Rio Road.A map of the 12K and 5K start and finish area will be available soon.

Expo

  • The 2017 Health & Fitness Expo is located in the Portola Hotel & Spa De Anza Ballroom at 2 Portola Plaza in Monterey and across the street from the Monterey Marriott. Dates and hours are Friday, April 28th from noon to 6:00 p.m. and Saturday, April 29th from 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.
  • At the Expo, participants in the Marathon, Relay, 21 and 10.6-Miler, the 12K, 5K and 3K will pick up their race bib with attached timing chip, gear check bag, official Race Program and event shirt. Be sure to bring photo ID (required for bib pick-up). We will have computers on site for bib look-up and the timing company will be sending bib information during the week prior to the Expo.

Free Clinic

  • There will be vendors, businesses and other race representatives at the Expo offering a variety of fitness apparel and equipment as well as providing health and fitness related demonstrations. Stay tuned for the list of 2017 vendors.
  • If you are a vendor and are interested in exhibiting at the Expo we will have information and the Expo Application available shortly.
  • A series of FREE race clinics will take place on Saturday, April 29th in the Portola Room located across the lobby from the Expo. Pasta Party tickets will be on sale for $25.
